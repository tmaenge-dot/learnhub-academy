ES Circle

large circle written in the same direction as S circle represents SES,
ZES or SEZ:

1 at the end of words

taxes, success, causes, _ losses, chooses, offices, bases,

services, passes, uses.
2. in the middle of some words

ecessary, necessity, excessive, successfully.

Any vowel other than the short e between the two s’s is indicated by
writing the vowel sign inside the circle:

eS a a i

basis, insist, exercise, emphasize, exhaust.
b A few words ending in s-s are written with the S circle and stroke S
for distinguishing purposes:

a ae . a cw

access but causes; excess but cases; possess but pauses.
‘ Downward L is written in: um... . necessarily.
