## ‚úÖ STROKES COMPLETED

### Unit 1 - Straight Downstrokes (6 consonants)
All 6 strokes are STRAIGHT LINES going downward:
- **pee** (P) - Light straight line slanting down to the LEFT
- **bee** (B) - Heavy straight line slanting down to the LEFT (THICKER than pee)
- **tee** (T) - Light straight VERTICAL line going down
- **dee** (D) - Heavy straight VERTICAL line going down (THICKER than tee)
- **chay** (CH) - Light straight line slanting down to the RIGHT
- **jay** (J) - Heavy straight line slanting down to the RIGHT (THICKER than chay)

### Unit 2 - Curved Strokes (8 consonants)
All curves written downwards:
- **ef** (F) - Light curve
- **vee** (V) - Heavy curve (DARKER than ef)
- **ith** (Th) - Light curve (thin sound)
- **thee** (TH) - Heavy curve (this sound, DARKER than ith)
- **ess** (S) - Light curve
- **zee** (Z) - Heavy curve (DARKER than ess)
- **ish** (Sh) - Light curve
- **zhee** (Zh) - Heavy curve (DARKER than ish)

### Unit 3 - Horizontal/Upward Strokes (5 consonants)
Written forward (left to right), light strokes except G and NG:
- **kay** (K) - Light horizontal STRAIGHT stroke
- **gay** (G) - Heavy horizontal STRAIGHT stroke (DARKER than kay)
- **em** (M) - Light curve stroke with inside curve ON the line
- **en** (N) - Light curve stroke curving AWAY from the line
- **ing** (NG) - Heavy curve stroke curving away from line (heavy N)

### Unit 7 - Stroke R (2 forms)
Two forms for easier joining and indicating vowel position:
- **ray** (Upward R) - Light upward stroke
  - Examples: are, our hour, should, your, year, satisfactory
- **ar** (Downward R) - Light downward stroke
  - Examples: think thanked
  - Used initially when preceded by a vowel

### Unit 11 - Halving
Strokes are halved to indicate a following T or D:
- **Halving for T** - Light strokes halved for following T
  - Examples: note, thought, let, melt, hoped, son, sight, stopped, talked, reached, left, checked, fight, part
- **Halving for D** - Light strokes halved for following D (only following T)
  - Examples: bad, dead, field, guide, changed, had, head, goods, nights, leads, thoughts
  - Note: The circle is always read last

### Unit 12 - R Hook
Small hook at beginning of strokes adds R (clockwise motion):
- **pr** (pee + R hook) - Examples: April, October, trial, address, weather, manager, increased, progress
- **br** (bee + R hook) - Examples: brother, bring, break
- **tr** (tee + R hook) - Examples: try, tree, true, trade
- **dr** (dee + R hook) - Examples: dry, dream, drive
- **chr** (chay + R hook) - Examples: chrome, chronic
- **jr** (jay + R hook) - Examples: jar, jury, germ
- **kr** (kay + R hook) - Examples: cry, credit, create
- **gr** (gay + R hook) - Examples: grow, great, green, agree

### Unit 13 - N Hook
Small final hook adds N:
- **N Hook on curves** - Written on inside of curves
  - Examples: often, phone, then, machine, salesman, learn
- **N Hook on straight strokes** - Written to the right (clockwise)
  - Examples: town, done, bulletin, foreign, gone, when
- **N Hook following R** - R is written upwards
  - Examples: turn, return, learn, western, corn, pattern
